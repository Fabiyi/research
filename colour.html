COLOUR PSYCHOLOGY

It is the study of how colour affect human emotion, behaviour, and perceptions. RED: Emotion (passion, energy, urgency, excitement, love); Behaviour (increase heart rate and sense of urgency);Usage (fast-food restaurants, clearance sales and symbols of warning) BLUE: Emotion (calmness, trust, stability, serenity);Behaviour (promote relaxation and sense of security);Usage(Banks, healthcare environment) YELLOW: Emotion (happiness, optimism, warmth);Behaviour (stimulate mental processes and energy); Usage(product and advertisement aimed at children , road signs) GREEN: Emotion (growth, health, tranquility, wealth); Behaviour (nature and tranquility);Usage(eco-friendly brands, healthcare and finance sectors) ORANGE: Emotion (enthusiasm, creativity, warmth); Behaviour (stimulate social interaction and activity, create a sense of urgency); Usage(calls to action, food and beverage industries. To highlight fun or energetic environment) PURPLE: Emotion (luxury, royalty, spirituality, mystery);Behaviour(stimulate imagination and convey a sense of luxury.); Usage(beauty products, luxury brands and spiritual products) BLACK: Emotion (power, elegance, sophistication to sadness or fear); Usage (high-end products, fashion, signify authority or elegance) WHITE: Emotion (purity, simplicity, cleanliness.); Behaviour(sense of space and calm); Usage (healthcare, tech products and minimalist designs) PINK: Emotion (compassion, nurturing, love, playfulness); Behaviour(femininity, feeling of calmness and romance); Usage( women and care & compassion) BROWN: Emotion (stability, reliability, warmth, comfort); Behaviour (sense of reliability and support, earthiness and natural materials); Usage (outdoor products, nature or organic products.)

VISUAL HIERARCHY

This is a way to organize information on a page so that the most important things stand out first, making it easy for people to find what need quickly.

KEY COMPONENTS OF VISUAL HIERARCHY

SIZE: It uses notice large elements more easily. COLOR: The bright color typically attract more attention than muted ones. CONTRAST: The dramatically contrasted colors are more eye-catching. ALIGNMENT: The out-of -alignment elements stand out over other aligned ones. REPETITION: The repeating style can suggest content is related. PROXIMITY: The closely placed elements seem related. WHITESPACE: The more space around elements draws the eye towards them. TEXTURE AND STYLE: The richer textures stand out over flat ones.

TYPOGRAPHY

It is the art and technique of arranging type (text) ina way that makes written language not legible but also visually appealing. It helps to convey messages in design communication.

ARIA
